About Me

Growing up in the middle of a transitioning world has been rewarding. At a young age I was introduced to technology, but not the kind like today. If I ever accidentally pressed the "Network" button on my flip phone, I frantically closed it, thinking that my parents were about to be billed hundred of dollars because I connected to the internet.

In college, once I took my first MIS class I knew that I had found my major. Programming, database creation, and cyber security were the classes I loved the most. They were all challenging, yet rewarding. From the months of August to December in 2016, I studied abroad in Arezzo, Italy and earned a International Area Studies minor while exploring Europe. During this time I taught Italian high school students basic American History, Native American culture, and English. It was there that I developed my leadership skills.
